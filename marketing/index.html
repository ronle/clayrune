How it works

Three steps and you're running.

Download & open.

Grab Mission Control from GitHub. It runs on your own machine — no account, no cloud, no data leaving your laptop.

Pick an assistant.

Choose from the starter pack (Party Planner, Research Intern, Market Watcher…) or write your own in a single file. Give it a name and an emoji and it's ready.

Answer when asked.

Your assistants work on their own. When one needs a decision, its card turns amber. Tap, answer, done.

Features

Built for humans. Quietly powerful.

👥

Named assistants

Each project is a named teammate with a face. "Party Planner" reads differently than "agent-04".

💬

Plain English status

Five states — Working, Needs you, Stuck, Done, Resting. No raw logs unless you ask.

🎨

Two tones, your way

Warm cream for daylight, Soft dark for late nights. Pick your accent, pick your density.

🗣️

Voice that fits you

Casual ("I need a quick answer") or Professional ("Awaiting input"). Same app, two personalities.

🧭

First-run tour

Opens with a sample "Birthday party planner" project. Try things without stakes, then clear it.

🔒

Runs on your machine

Local-first. No telemetry. Your data never leaves your laptop unless you ship it somewhere yourself.

🛠️

Advanced mode

One toggle reveals the power-user dashboard: streams, plan approvals, tokens, schedules, memory.

🧩

Custom assistants

Define your own in a single file. Hot-reload. No SDK, no boilerplate.

❤️

Open source

MIT licensed. Fork it, rebrand it, improve it. Help shape what "friendly agents" mean.

FAQ

Questions people ask.

Do I need to know how to code?
No. The friendly view is designed for PMs, designers, ops people, and anyone who wants to run AI assistants without touching a terminal. If you've used a to-do app, you'll find your way around.
Where does my data go?
Nowhere you don't send it. Mission Control runs locally on your machine. Your assistants talk to whichever AI provider you configure, and the conversations stay between your computer and that provider.
Is it really free?
Yes. Mission Control is MIT-licensed open source. You'll pay your AI provider for the model calls your assistants make (OpenAI, Anthropic, or a local model), but the app itself is free forever.
Can I make my own assistants?
Yes, and it's meant to be easy. An assistant is a single config file — a name, an emoji, a prompt, and what tools it can use. No SDK to learn. Copy an existing one and modify.
I'm a developer — where's the old dashboard?
Still there. Toggle Advanced mode from Settings and you get the original Mission Control: raw agent streams, plan approval, token counters, schedules, GitHub issues sync, and the rest.
How can I contribute?
The whole thing is on GitHub. Open an issue, send a PR, write a custom assistant, improve the docs, or translate the copy. The project's still young — even small contributions help a lot.
