Manifesto

AI tools should
feel like teammates.

Not dashboards of tokens. Not terminals. Not walls of streaming text. Teammates.

Most agent tools today are built for the people who wrote them — developers, staring at streams of JSON, counting tokens, approving plans. That's fine. Some of us love that.

But every one of us has a friend, a parent, a colleague who could use an assistant too. Someone who isn't going to open a terminal. Someone who'd really like a little help with the birthday party, the newsletter, the research, the inbox.

Every bit of jargon we hide is a person we invite in.

Mission Control started as a developer tool and stayed one for a long time. This redesign is an experiment: what if the default view wasn't the dashboard — it was a warm, visual team of assistants that talked to you in plain English? What if the developer dashboard was one toggle away, not the front door?

We're betting this is how more people end up with AI assistants actually helping them. Not through better models or smarter prompts. Through UIs that don't make them feel dumb.

Mission Control is open source, MIT-licensed, local-first, and stays that way. No company owns it. No user data leaves your laptop. Every improvement comes from people who use it.

If you want to help — build a custom assistant, fix a typo, translate a tooltip, tell a friend — you're already on the team.

— Ron & the Mission Control contributors

Our values

What we optimize for.

🤗

Welcoming by default

If a non-developer feels lost in the first 30 seconds, we've failed. Every label, every button gets tested with that in mind.

🔒

Local & private

Your projects, conversations, and files stay on your machine. Mission Control has no servers, no telemetry, no accounts.

🧱

Powerful underneath

Friendliness isn't the same as dumbed-down. Every pro feature — streams, schedules, memory — is still one toggle away.

🌱

Small and forkable

Readable codebase, single-file configs, no mandatory SDKs. If you want to take it somewhere new, we want to help you.

Come build with us.

The project's young and the community's small. There's room for your fingerprints on every page.
